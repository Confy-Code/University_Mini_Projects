## 1. INSURANCE CLAIM HANDLING
Based on the scenario below, create a `Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) diagram` that represents the motor insurance claim handling process.
The process begins when a customer submits a motor insurance claim together with the required supporting documents. **The Notification Department** at the insurance company reviews the submitted documents for completeness and registers the claim.
Once the claim is registered, the Handling Department receives the claim, verifies the insurance details, and performs an assessment.
After the assessment, a decision is made:
- If the assessment is approved, the garage is contacted to authorize the repairs, and the payment is scheduled in that order.
- If the assessment is not approved, the claim is rejected. 
In both cases, whether the claim is approved or rejected, an outcome letter is sent to the customer, and the process ends.

## 2. BPMN MODELING SCENARIO: ORDER CONFIRMATION AND CHANGE MANAGEMENT PROCESS
A SUPPLIER HAS APPROVED A PURCHASE ORDER SUBMITTED BY A RETAILER AND SENDS AN APPROVAL NOTIFICATION TO THE RETAILER. UPON RECEIVING THE NOTIFICATION, THE RETAILER IS EXPECTED TO RESPOND WITHIN 48 HOURS. THE RETAILER MAY CHOOSE TO:
- CONFIRM THE ORDER,
- REQUEST MODIFICATIONS TO THE ORDER,
- CANCEL THE ORDER, OR
- PROVIDE NO RESPONSE.
IF THE RETAILER SENDS AN ORDER CONFIRMATION WITHIN 48 HOURS, THE SUPPLIER PROCEEDS WITH THE NORMAL ORDER FULFILLMENT PROCESS.
IF THE RETAILER REQUESTS AN ORDER MODIFICATION, THE SUPPLIER UPDATES THE ORDER DETAILS AND SENDS THE REVISED ORDER BACK TO THE RETAILER FOR CONFIRMATION. THE RETAILER MAY REQUEST MODIFICATIONS MULTIPLE TIMES; HOWEVER, THE SUPPLIER ALLOWS A MAXIMUM OF THREE CHANGE REQUESTS. IF A FOURTH CHANGE REQUEST IS RECEIVED, THE SUPPLIER AUTOMATICALLY CANCELS THE ORDER.
IF THE RETAILER SENDS AN ORDER CANCELLATION AT ANY TIME DURING THE CONFIRMATION PROCESS, THE SUPPLIER IMMEDIATELY CANCELS THE ORDER.
IF THE SUPPLIER DOES NOT RECEIVE ANY RESPONSE FROM THE RETAILER WITHIN 48 HOURS, THE ORDER IS AUTOMATICALLY CANCELED.
WHENEVER AN ORDER IS CANCELED-WHETHER DUE TO RETAILER CANCELLATION, EXCEEDING THE MAXIMUM NUMBER OF CHANGES, OR LACK OF RESPONSE THE SUPPLIER SENDS A CUSTOMER FEEDBACK QUESTIONNAIRE TO THE RETAILER BEFORE CLOSING THE PROCESS.
TASK
USING BUSINESS PROCESS MODEL AND NOTATION (BPMN), MODEL THE ABOVE PROCESS. YOUR DIAGRAM SHOULD CLEARLY SHOW:
THE SUPPLIER AND RETAILER AS SEPARATE PARTICIPANTS (POOLS OR LANES).
MESSAGE EXCHANGES BETWEEN THE SUPPLIER AND RETAILER.
THE 48-HOUR WAITING PERIOD.
DECISION POINTS FOR CONFIRMATION, MODIFICATION, CANCELLATION, AND NO RESPONSE.
THE LOOP FOR HANDLING ORDER CHANGES.
THE RULE LIMITING MODIFICATIONS TO A MAXIMUM OF THREE REQUESTS.
THE CUSTOMER FEEDBACK QUESTIONNAIRE PROCESS FOLLOWING ANY ORDER CANCELLATION.
